The Christmas Carol

Charles Dickens

"A Christmas Carol" by Charles Dickens is a novella about Ebenezer Scrooge, a miserly and cold-hearted old man. On Christmas Eve, Scrooge is visited by the ghost of his former business partner, Marley, who warns him of a similar fate if he doesn't change his ways. Throughout the night, Scrooge is visited by three spirits who show him the past, present,and future, leading to his transformation and newfound generosity

Hobbit

J. R. R. Tolkien

Bilbo Baggins, a hobbit living a quiet life in the Shire, is suddenly thrust into an adventure when Gandalf the wizard and a group of dwarves arrive at his doorstep. They convince him to join them on a quest to reclaim the Lonely Mountain and its treasure from the dragon, Smaug. Throughout the journey, Bilbo encounters trolls, goblins, elves, giant spiders, and more. He also finds a mysterious ring that grants him the power of invisibility, which becomes crucial to their mission. Despite his initial reluctance,Bilbo grows in courage and resourcefulness.
